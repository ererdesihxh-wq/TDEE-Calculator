Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the TDEE calculator?

Our TDEE calculator uses scientifically validated formulas (Mifflin-St Jeor and Katch-McArdle) to provide estimates. However, all TDEE calculations are estimates, not measurements. Individual results may vary by 10-20% based on genetics, hormone levels, and other factors. For more information, see our Accuracy Guide.

Can you provide personalized diet or exercise plans?

No. TheTDEEcalculator.net provides educational information and tools only. We do not create personalized diet or exercise plans. For personalized guidance, please consult with a registered dietitian, certified personal trainer, or healthcare provider.

I found an error in your content. How do I report it?

We appreciate when users help us maintain accurate information. Please email contact@thetdeecalculator.net with the subject line "Content Correction" and include the page URL, the incorrect information, and the correct information with sources if possible.

Can I use your content on my website or in my research?

Our content is protected by copyright. For permission to use our content, please contact us with details about how you intend to use it. We generally allow educational and non-commercial use with proper attribution. Commercial use requires explicit permission.

Do you have an API for your calculator?

We do not currently offer a public API. If you're interested in integrating our calculator functionality into your application, please contact our partnerships team at partnerships@thetdeecalculator.net.
